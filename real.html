So Why is Thermochemistry Useful?

You might be wondering how thermochemistry is used outside of burning cheesepuffs in a calorimeter. Well, let me assure you that thermo is extremely important in many engineering projects! You can refer to the house-construction lab, or look at the few examples we've listed below.



Do you ever wonder why coldpacks are cold? An instant cold pack consists of a bag containing solid ammonium nitrate and a second bag of water. When the bag of water is broken, the pack becomes cold because the dissolution of ammonium nitrate is an endothermic process that removes thermal energy from the water. The cold pack then removes thermal energy from your body.




Another example is car companies. When designing mileage devices, car companies use Hess' law and see how much heat is released to measure how much gasoline has been used! Just remember that Hess' law is relevant when you drive every day! Car companies also use water to cool engines. Because the water will evaporate and absorb the heat, it is an effective method for ensuring that the engine doesn't overheat.
